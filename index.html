About Me

Hello! I am Roshan Rane, currently a Research Associate and a Ph.D. student at the "Machine Learning in Clinical Neuroimaging" group. I received my bachelor's degree in engineering in 2014 from Bangalore Institute of Technology, India, and completed my master's in Cognitive Systems from Universität Potsdam in 2020. In my master's, my research focus was deep learning, computer vision, and cognitive modeling.

Apart from my research experiences (see publications link below), I also have substantial industrial experience. After my bachelor's, I worked as an embedded software engineer for three years at Robert Bosch, India. During my master's, I also worked as a research assistant in this lab where I worked closely with brain MRI analysis and later on worked as a Machine Learning Specialist at an autonomous driving startup in Berlin for a year. My current research in this lab involves developing deep learning methods for analyzing brain MRI of subjects with psychiatric disorders like drug addiction and drawing reliable conclusions. I am also interested in designing AI-based tools to assist in the diagnosis of Psychiatric disorders.
